## 2. Descriptions

### 2a. App Store — full description (~4,000 char target)

> **Petwell is a calm, trustworthy companion for your pet's everyday health.** Keep daily care on track, log what you notice, build one clear health history, and walk into your vet visit prepared — without the hype.
>
> Petwell is built on a simple promise: we tell you what we actually know, and we're honest about what we don't. Petwell offers information and guidance to help you and your veterinarian — it does not diagnose, and it is not a substitute for professional veterinary care or an emergency service.
>
> **DAILY CARE, ON AUTOPILOT**
> A clean "Today" hub shows your pet's care checklist, recent changes, and what's coming up. Set reminders for meals, medications, refills, vaccines, and appointments so nothing slips.
>
> **ADAPTIVE SYMPTOM GUIDANCE (NOT A DIAGNOSIS)**
> Answer a few adaptive questions about a symptom and Petwell walks you through **possible causes** and sensible next steps — what to watch, what to log, and when to call your vet. It never names a diagnosis or doses medication. If your answers suggest an emergency or possible poisoning, Petwell stops and routes you straight to professional help.
>
> **ONE HEALTH TIMELINE**
> Every log, photo, record, and reminder builds a longitudinal history you can actually use. Track appetite, stool, itching, activity, and weight over time, watch for patterns that emerge from your own logs, and keep vaccines, documents, and visit notes in one place.
>
> **FOOD INTELLIGENCE, GRADED BY EVIDENCE**
> Look up a pet food and see how strong the evidence behind it really is — graded from independent product-level lab reports down to "brand claim" and "no public test found." Petwell checks the FDA's official recall feed, looks at ingredient transparency, AAFCO life-stage fit, and brand transparency, and distinguishes a brand-level signal from your exact product. When no public, product-level Certificate of Analysis exists, Petwell says so plainly — "No public product-level COA found" — instead of guessing. We never call a food "cleanest," "purest," or "safest," and a photo never raises a purity score. A label scan reads ingredients; it can't detect heavy metals, pesticides, or contaminants.
>
> **VET-READY REPORTS**
> Turn your pet's history into a clear, shareable summary for your veterinarian — what changed, what you've been tracking, and the questions to ask. Exporting your data is always free, whether or not you subscribe.
>
> **SAFETY THAT KNOWS ITS LIMITS**
> A built-in toxin reference and one-tap hotlines mean an emergency is never "handled in the app." Suspected poisoning or severe signs route you to your vet, an emergency clinic, the ASPCA Animal Poison Control Center, or the Pet Poison Helpline — fast.
>
> **PRIVACY YOU CONTROL**
> Your pet's health data is yours. Export everything as JSON anytime (free), delete stored scan photos, or delete your account and data outright. With an account, your records are isolated to you. We do not sell your pet's photos or health data, we don't show pay-to-rank recommendations, and we don't use your content to train AI models.
>
> **OPTIONAL AI, OFF BY DEFAULT**
> Petwell's AI helpers are entirely opt-in. When you turn them on in Settings, they can explain a result in plain language or help summarize a record or report — always as a draft you review, never as a source of truth for urgent safety. Document processing is a separate opt-in, and no AI model key ever ships in the app.
>
> **PETWELL PRO**
> The core app — profiles, reminders, symptom triage, food checks, full records, and free report export — is free. Petwell Pro unlocks more: unlimited scans, advanced Ask follow-ups, trend correlations, wearable integrations, advanced vet reports, and multi-pet family sharing. Honest pricing, no dark patterns, cancel anytime. Pro is available as a monthly, yearly, or lifetime purchase via the App Store.
>
> Petwell is for adults caring for their pets. It provides information and guidance only — not veterinary advice — and is not for emergencies. Always consult your veterinarian for diagnosis and treatment.

## 4. Category & age/content rating

### 4a. Category recommendation

- **Primary (both stores): Medical.** Petwell's core is pet health information, symptom guidance, records, and vet handoff — that fits Medical better than Lifestyle, and it sets honest user expectations (informational, vet-adjacent).
- **Secondary (App Store allows two): Health & Fitness** (or *Lifestyle*).
- **If Medical feels too clinical for a pet-owner audience,** Health & Fitness is a defensible primary. Pick one and keep the "informational guidance, not a diagnosis, not a medical device" framing consistent with the review notes (§9). Do not select a category that implies professional/clinical veterinary services.

### 4b. Age & content rating guidance

- **Apple age rating:** request **17+** if you complete the App Privacy/age questionnaire conservatively for a health-information app, or **12+** at minimum. The app is intended for adults and includes infrequent/mild references to medical and health topics. It contains **no** user-generated social feeds, no gambling, no objectionable content.
- **Google Play / IARC questionnaire:** complete it honestly — expect **Everyone** or **Teen** depending on how you answer the medical-information questions. Answer "yes" to references to medical topics; "no" to violence, sexual content, gambling, and user-to-user communication.
- **Target audience:** adults (pet owners). State **not directed at children under 13** (matches the in-app privacy policy). Do not opt into Play's "Designed for Families" program.
- Declare the app's **disclaimers** (not veterinary advice, not for emergencies) in the description and in review notes so reviewers see the positioning immediately.

## 8. App Review notes (Apple App Review & Google Play review)

Paste into App Store Connect "App Review Information → Notes" and Play Console "App content / review notes." Provide a demo account if the build requires sign-in.

> **What Petwell is:** An informational pet-health companion (daily care, reminders, adaptive symptom guidance, health timeline, food evidence lookup, vet-ready reports). It provides guidance only — it does **not** diagnose, is **not** a medical device, and is **not** for emergencies. Emergencies and suspected poisoning route out to a veterinarian, emergency clinic, the ASPCA Animal Poison Control Center (888-426-4435), and the Pet Poison Helpline (855-764-7661) via one-tap calling — never handled in-app.
>
> **Trying the app without your own data (demo mode):** No account is required to explore. Use the explicit **"Try a demo profile"** action — available on the first-pet screen and in **Settings** — to load clearly-labeled sample pets. Demo pets and any sample data carry a visible **DEMO** badge. A normal install starts empty (no pets) and prompts you to add your first pet; production builds never auto-load demo data.
>
> **Enabling AI features (optional, off by default):** All AI helpers are **OFF** until the user turns them on. To test them, go to **Settings → AI** and enable AI; document processing is a **separate** opt-in toggle. With AI off, every AI button shows a friendly "turn on AI in Settings" note and makes no network call. AI is informational and assistive (explain/summarize/rewrite as a reviewable draft) — it never diagnoses, prescribes, or overrides the emergency/poison routing (the safety banner is deterministic and shows regardless of model output).
>
> **AI keys are server-side:** No model API key ships in the app. AI requests go to Supabase Edge Functions that call the provider (OpenAI) with a server-only secret. If AI is not deployed/keyed in the review environment, AI buttons return a graceful "AI is currently turned off" message — this is expected, not an error.
>
> **Subscriptions (Petwell Pro):** Pro uses native in-app purchases via RevenueCat (monthly / yearly / lifetime), with **Restore Purchases** and **Manage subscription** in the app. To test purchases, use a **sandbox Apple ID** (iOS) or a **license-tested account on a Play test track** (Android); real charges are not incurred in sandbox. The core app and data export remain free without a subscription. On platforms where IAP isn't available (e.g. web), the app shows a graceful "subscriptions available in the iOS/Android app" state.
>
> **Privacy:** Users can export all data as JSON (free), delete stored scan photos, and delete their account and data in-app. We don't sell health data, show ads, or use data for tracking; crash reporting runs with PII disabled.
>
> **Food claims are intentionally conservative:** Petwell grades food by evidence strength and openly says "No public product-level COA found" when no independent lab report exists. It never labels a food "clean," "cleanest," "purest," "safest," or "lab-verified," and a photo/label scan never raises a purity score. Some areas (e.g. marketplace/telehealth) are clearly labeled "research preview"/"coming soon" and expose only real actions.
